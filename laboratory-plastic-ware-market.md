<p><p>The laboratory plasticware market features various material types, each with specific properties. HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene) is durable and resistant to chemicals. LDPE (Low-Density Polyethylene) offers flexibility and ease of sealing. PP (Polypropylene) provides heat resistance and is widely used in lab containers. PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride) is cost-effective and versatile. PETG (Polyethylene Terephthalate Glycol) is transparent and robust. PC (Polycarbonate) is impact-resistant. PS (Polystyrene) is lightweight and economical, while FEP (Fluorinated Ethylene Propylene) and PFA (Perfluoroalkoxy) exhibit excellent chemical resistance and thermal stability.</p></p>
