<p><p>The hygienic nonwoven fabric market is segmented into several types. Spunlaced fabrics are created through mechanical entanglement, providing softness and strength, making them suitable for personal care products. Heat sealing fabrics employ thermal bonding, offering durability and water resistance, ideal for medical applications. Dry papermaking utilizes a process that yields high absorbency and functionality, often used in wipes. Meltblown fabrics are formed by melting polymers and blowing them into fine filaments, commonly used for filtration and protective clothing. Other types include specialized nonwovens for niche applications.</p></p>
