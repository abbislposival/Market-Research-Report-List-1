<p><p>Hollow fiber membrane filters are categorized into microfiltration, ultrafiltration, and nanofiltration, each serving specific separation needs. Microfiltration removes larger particles and microorganisms, making it suitable for water treatment and food processing. Ultrafiltration targets smaller solutes, effectively separating proteins and macromolecules, commonly used in wastewater and dairy industries. Nanofiltration, with even tighter pores, is effective for softening water and removing divalent ions and organic compounds, finding applications in various sectors, including pharmaceuticals and food industries.</p></p>
