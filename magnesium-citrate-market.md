<p><p>The magnesium citrate market primarily consists of two types: anhydrous and nonahydrate. The anhydrous type is a dry form, typically used for pharmaceutical applications due to its higher concentration of magnesium and better stability. In contrast, the nonahydrate type contains water molecules, making it more suitable for dietary supplements and food applications, as it is easier to digest and absorb. Both types serve distinct purposes across various industries, influencing their demand and pricing dynamics in the market.</p></p>
