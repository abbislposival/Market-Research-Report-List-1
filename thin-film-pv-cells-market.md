<p><p>Thin film photovoltaic (PV) cells are a type of solar technology that utilizes a thin layer of photovoltaic material to convert sunlight into electricity. Unlike traditional silicon-based solar cells, thin film cells can be made from various materials such as cadmium telluride, amorphous silicon, and copper indium gallium selenide. Their lightweight and flexible nature makes them suitable for diverse applications, including building-integrated photovoltaics and portable devices.</p><p>The Thin Film PV Cells Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 10.10% during the forecast period, driven by increasing demand for renewable energy sources, advancements in technology, and rising environmental concerns. Governments worldwide are implementing supportive policies and incentives to promote the adoption of solar energy, contributing to market expansion. </p><p>Latest trends in the market include ongoing research and development efforts aimed at improving the efficiency and lifespan of thin film solar cells, as well as the integration of energy storage solutions with these systems.
