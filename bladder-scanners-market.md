<p><p>Bladder scanners are categorized into two primary market types: 2D and 3D. 2D bladder scanners utilize two-dimensional imaging to assess bladder volume and detect conditions such as urinary retention. These devices are generally more compact and cost-effective. In contrast, 3D bladder scanners create three-dimensional images, providing a comprehensive view of bladder anatomy and volume, leading to enhanced diagnostic accuracy. The choice between 2D and 3D depends on the clinical requirements, budget constraints, and desired imaging detail.</p></p>
