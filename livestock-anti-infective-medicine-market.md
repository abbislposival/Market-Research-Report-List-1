<p><p>The livestock anti-infective medicine market is divided into two primary categories: external use and internal use. External use medications are applied topically to treat infections on the skin or wounds, aiding in rapid healing and preventing the spread of pathogens. Internal use medicines, on the other hand, are administered orally or via injection to combat systemic infections and support overall animal health. Both types are crucial for maintaining herd vitality and productivity in livestock management.</p></p>
