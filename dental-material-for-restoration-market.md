<p><p>The dental restoration market comprises three primary material types: ceramics, amalgam, and composites. Ceramics, known for their aesthetic appeal and durability, are used in crowns and veneers. Amalgam, a durable alloy of metals, is favored for posterior fillings due to its strength and longevity. Composites, made from resin, offer a tooth-colored option, making them ideal for anterior fillings and cosmetic repairs. Each material is selected based on factors like location, functionality, and patient preference, ensuring effective dental restorations.</p></p>
