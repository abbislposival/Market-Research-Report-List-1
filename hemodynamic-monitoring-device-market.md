<p><ul><li>Invasive</li><li>Minimally Invasive</li><li>Non-invasive</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Hemodynamic monitoring devices are classified into three main types based on their invasiveness. Invasive devices involve placing sensors directly into blood vessels for accurate measurements, typically used in critically ill patients. Minimally invasive devices, on the other hand, provide essential hemodynamic data with reduced risk by using peripheral access or advanced non-intrusive methods like monitors that estimate central pressures. Non-invasive devices offer the least risk, utilizing techniques such as Doppler ultrasound and oscillometric methods to assess cardiovascular function without penetrating the skin.</p></p>
