<p><ul><li>PIR (Passive Infrared) Sensors</li><li>Ultrasonic Sensors</li><li>Dual-technology Occupancy Sensors</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Occupancy sensors are integral to lighting systems, optimizing energy efficiency and enhancing convenience. Passive Infrared (PIR) sensors detect heat emitted by individuals, making them ideal for static environments. Ultrasonic sensors utilize sound waves to sense movement, allowing them to detect occupants in dynamic or larger spaces. Dual-technology occupancy sensors combine PIR and ultrasonic technologies for increased accuracy, minimizing false activations. Other sensor types may include microwave sensors and photoelectric sensors, each serving specific applications to further enhance energy savings and user experience.</p></p>
