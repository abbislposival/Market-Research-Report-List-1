<p><p>Apertured films are categorized into two main market types: monolayer and laminated films. Monolayer films consist of a single layer of material, providing simplicity and cost-effectiveness, often used in basic packaging applications. In contrast, laminated films are composed of multiple layers, enhancing barrier properties and durability, making them suitable for more demanding packaging needs. This distinction allows manufacturers to choose the appropriate type based on specific product requirements, such as moisture resistance, strength, and shelf life.</p></p>
