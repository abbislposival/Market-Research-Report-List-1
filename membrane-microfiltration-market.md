<p><p>The membrane microfiltration market can be divided into two main types: organic and inorganic membranes. Organic membranes, typically made from polymeric materials, offer flexibility and ease of use, making them suitable for various applications like water treatment and food processing. In contrast, inorganic membranes, often constructed from ceramic or metal materials, provide superior thermal and chemical stability, making them ideal for challenging environments, such as wastewater treatment and pharmaceutical applications. Both types play crucial roles in enhancing filtration efficiency and performance.</p></p>
