<p><p>A Variable Reluctance Sensor (VRS) is an electromagnetic device that detects changes in magnetic fields to determine speed, position, or displacement. It operates on the principle of varying reluctance within a magnetic circuit, typically utilizing a core and coil arrangement that generates a voltage signal proportional to the change in position of a target object. VRS is widely used in automotive applications, such as in speed sensing for wheels and crankshafts, as well as in industrial machinery for position feedback.</p><p>The Variable Reluctance Sensor Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.40% during the forecast period. Innovations in sensor technologies and the rising trend of automation further accelerate market expansion. Additionally, the growing focus on electric and hybrid vehicles presents new opportunities for VRS implementation in advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS). The shift towards Industry 4.0 and the integration of Internet of Things (IoT) technologies enhance the role of variable reluctance sensors, ensuring they remain a critical component in modern engineering applications.</p></p>
