<p><p>3D Arterial Spin Labeling (3D ASL) is a non-invasive MRI technique used to measure cerebral blood flow by utilizing magnetically labeled arterial blood. It includes two main market types: Continuous Arterial Spin Labeling (CASL) and Pulsed Arterial Spin Labeling (PASL). CASL employs continuous labeling of arterial blood flow, providing high signal-to-noise ratios, while PASL uses pulsed labeling, enabling simpler and more flexible imaging protocols. Both methods are vital for neurological assessments and research, particularly in studying brain function and disorders.</p></p>
