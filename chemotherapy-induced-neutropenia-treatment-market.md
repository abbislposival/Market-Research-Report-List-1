<p><p>The chemotherapy-induced neutropenia treatment market comprises various therapeutic approaches. Antibiotic therapy focuses on preventing or treating infections due to decreased white blood cell counts. Colony-stimulating factor therapy stimulates bone marrow to produce more neutrophils, enhancing immunity. Granulocyte transfusion involves infusing neutrophils from healthy donors to bolster the patient’s immune response. Splenectomy may be performed in specific cases to improve blood cell counts by removing the spleen. Other treatments include natural supplements and supportive care strategies for managing symptoms.</p></p>
