Recuperative cycles utilize heat exchangers to recycle heat, improving efficiency, while regenerative cycles involve a working gas that absorbs and releases heat through a regenerative heat exchanger, enhancing cooling capacity. Other market types may include innovative designs and hybrid systems that combine features of both types, catering to diverse applications in various industries such as aerospace, medical, and electronics cooling.</p></p>
