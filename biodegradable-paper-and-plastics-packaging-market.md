<p><p>The biodegradable paper and plastics packaging market primarily includes two types: polylactic acid (PLA) and polyhydroxyalkanoates (PHA). PLA, derived from renewable resources like corn starch, is widely used for packaging due to its good barrier properties and ability to decompose in industrial composting facilities. PHA, produced by microbial fermentation, is an emerging alternative known for its biodegradability in various environments. Both materials aim to reduce environmental impact, offering sustainable solutions in packaging while appealing to eco-conscious consumers.</p></p>
