<p><ul><li>DC Charger</li><li>AC Charger</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The residential EV charger market includes DC and AC chargers. DC chargers provide direct current, enabling faster charging by converting AC to DC within the charger. They are ideal for quick top-ups but are costlier and require higher power capacity. AC chargers use alternating current, relying on the vehicle's onboard converter, making them slower but more affordable and widely compatible. AC chargers dominate residential use due to lower costs and sufficient charging speeds for overnight needs, while DC chargers are less common in homes.</p></p>
