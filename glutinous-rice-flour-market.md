<p><p>The glutinous rice flour market consists primarily of two types: dried glutinous rice flour and water milled glutinous rice flour. Dried glutinous rice flour is produced by grinding dried glutinous rice, resulting in a fine powder used in various culinary applications, enabling elasticity in recipes like dumplings and rice cakes. Water milled glutinous rice flour, on the other hand, is made by soaking and grinding glutinous rice with water, offering a smoother texture, often preferred in traditional Asian desserts and dishes.</p></p>
